## 🔬 PART I: EVIDENCE-BASED FOUNDATION

### ESCAPE-MeVO Trial Failure (Feb 2025)
- **Finding:** Thrombectomy provided no added clinical benefit over best medical management for MeVO/DVO strokes
- **Trials:** ESCAPE-MeVO + DISTAL + DISCOUNT all negative
- **Clinical Takeaway:** "Do not support routine endovascular treatment of acute ischaemic stroke due to medium-vessel occlusion"

### Market Impact Assessment
| Segment | Impact | Rationale |
|---------|--------|-----------|
| **Large Vessel Occlusion (LVO)** | ✅ Unchanged | Strong evidence base intact |
| **Medium Vessel Occlusion (MeVO)** | 🔴 Major contraction | Trial failure removes indication |
| **Distal Vessel Occlusion (DVO)** | 🔴 Effectively dead | No evidence, no reimbursement |

## 🎯 PART IV: SYNTHESIS & STRATEGIC OPPORTUNITIES

### The Breakthrough Insight

**ESCAPE-MeVO didn't fail because MeVO is untreatable.**
**It failed because we asked the wrong question.**

The question wasn't: *"Can we do thrombectomy in MeVO?"*
The question should have been: *"How do we prevent MeVO stroke progression?"*

| Approach | Evidence-Based | Dream-State | Disruption Potential | Timeline |

| **Clot Stabilization** | ⚪ Unproven | 🌟 Radical | 9/10 | 10-15 years |

| **Biological Thrombectomy** | ⚪ Unproven | 🌟 Moonshot | 10/10 | 15-20 years |
| **Rigid Direct Access** | ⚪ Unproven | 🌟 Counter-intuitive | 8/10 | 5-8 years |
| **Predictive Prevention** | ⚪ Unproven | 🌟 Ultimate Upstream | 10/10 | 10-15 years |

### The Hybrid Opportunity (Recommended for Development)

**"The MeVO Stabilization System"**

**Core Concept:**
- Don't remove the clot
- Prevent it from causing harm
- Let the brain's natural collaterals compensate

**Technical Approach:**
1. Rapid MeVO detection (AI + advanced imaging)
2. Clot stabilization (ultrasonic or pharmacological)
3. Collateral enhancement (blood pressure management + neuroprotective agents)
4. Monitoring (non-invasive vascular imaging)

**Why This Works:**
- Avoids the "flexibility problem" entirely
- No need for distal navigation
- Leverages brain's natural compensatory mechanisms
- Lower risk than mechanical thrombectomy

**Regulatory Pathway:** 510(k) as adjunct to medical management

**Market:** All MeVO patients currently receiving "best medical management"
