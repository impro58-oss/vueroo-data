## Innovation Opportunities Matrix

### **OPPORTUNITY 1: Surface-Modified Aspiration Catheters**
**Concept:** Anti-thrombogenic coating applied to aspiration catheter interiors to reduce clot fragmentation and improve capture efficiency

**Technical Approach:**
- Endothelium-inspired coating with heparin-modulated NO release
- Nanostructured surface mimicking glycocalyx
- Photocatalytic TiO2 coating for thrombus adhesion prevention

**Market Gap:**
- Current aspiration catheters: bare metal or hydrophilic coating only
- No anti-thrombogenic coating technology in neurovascular access devices
- Competition focused on lumen size, not surface interaction

**Competitive Positioning:**
- Perfuze: Corrugated architecture (no coating)
- Toro: Large bore (no coating)
- Penumbra: Trackability focus (no coating)
- **Our Play:** "Capture and hold" — superior thrombus retention

**Regulatory Path:** 510(k) clearance (predicate: existing aspiration catheters)
**Timeline:** 12-18 months to market
**Near-term Viable:** ✅ YES — coating partnership + catheter OEM

**Risk:** Coating delamination, biocompatibility validation required

---

### **OPPORTUNITY 2: Fibrin-Rich Clot Disruption Technology**
**Concept:** Combined aspiration + intravascular lithotripsy (IVL) for "hard clots" resistant to current devices

**Technical Approach:**
- Piezoelectric transducers integrated into catheter tip
- Sonic pressure waves to disrupt fibrin-rich matrix
- Coupled with aspiration for immediate removal

**Market Gap:**
- Fibrin-rich clots have high failure rate with current devices (20-30%)
- No dedicated hard-clot technology in neurovascular
- IVL proven in peripheral/cardiac (Shockwave Medical) but not neuro

**Competitive Positioning:**
- Wallaby/Phenox: Stent retrievers
- Stryker: Trevo
- **Our Play:** "Softening before capture" — addresses resistant clots

**Regulatory Path:** De Novo pathway (no predicate in neurovascular)
**Timeline:** 24-36 months
**Near-term Viable:** ⚠️ MEDIUM — requires significant R&D

**Risk:** Vessel damage from sonic energy, distal embolization

---

### **OPPORTUNITY 3: Bioactive Flow Diverter Coating**
**Concept:** Pro-healing surface coating on flow diverter stents to accelerate endothelialization and reduce thrombosis risk

**Technical Approach:**
- VEGF-impregnated biodegradable polymer coating
- Nanotopography promoting endothelial cell adhesion
- Controlled release over 30-60 days

**Market Gap:**
- Flow diverters: 5-10% thrombosis rate in first year
- 12-24 months for complete healing
- No bioactive coatings in neurovascular stents

**Competitive Positioning:**
- Pipeline Medical: Bare metal flow diverters
- Medtronic: Pipeline Flex
- **Our Play:** "Accelerated healing" — faster endothelialization

**Regulatory Path:** PMA pathway (high-risk implant)
**Timeline:** 3-5 years
**Near-term Viable:** ❌ NO — long regulatory path

**Risk:** Intracranial stent = highest regulatory burden

---

### **OPPORTUNITY 4: Prehospital Stroke AI Triage Platform**
**Concept:** Wearable/portable device for LVO detection in ambulance before hospital arrival

**Technical Approach:**
- Optical blood flow monitoring (NIRS or photoplethysmography)
- AI algorithm trained on LVO signatures
- Integration with EMS dispatch systems

**Market Gap:**
- "Time is brain" — every minute = 1.9M neurons lost
- Current triage: symptom-based (FAST/BE-FAST)
- No physiological LVO detection in prehospital setting

**Competitive Positioning:**
- RapidAI/Viz.ai: Hospital-based imaging AI
- **Our Play:** "Bring the diagnosis forward" — ambulance LVO detection

**Regulatory Path:** 510(k) or De Novo
**Timeline:** 18-30 months
**Near-term Viable:** ✅ YES — software + sensor integration

**Risk:** False positives causing unnecessary transfers

---

### **OPPORTUNITY 5: Neuroprotection Infusion Catheter**
**Concept:** Multi-lumen catheter delivering neuroprotective agent during/after thrombectomy to protect penumbra during reperfusion

**Technical Approach:**
- Coaxial catheter: thrombectomy lumen + drug infusion lumen
- Localized neuroprotectant delivery (edaravone, uric acid)
- Targeting ischemic penumbra during procedure

**Market Gap:**
- No approved neuroprotective agents for acute stroke
- Reperfusion injury causes secondary damage
- All current devices focus on mechanical removal only

**Competitive Positioning:**
- All major players: Mechanical-only approach
- **Our Play:** "Protect while you extract" — combination therapy

**Regulatory Path:** Complex — device + drug combination
**Timeline:** 4-6 years
**Near-term Viable:** ❌ NO — requires drug partnership + clinical trials

**Risk:** Combination product pathway is lengthy

---

### **OPPORTUNITY 6: Thrombus Characterization Sensor**
**Concept:** Aspiration catheter with integrated sensors to identify clot composition (RBC vs. fibrin vs. WBC) in real-time

**Technical Approach:**
- Optical spectroscopy or impedance sensing at catheter tip
- AI classification of clot composition
- Guides device selection (stent retriever vs. aspiration)

**Market Gap:**
- "Blind" thrombectomy — no real-time clot assessment
- Device selection based on experience, not data
- High variability in first-pass success

**Competitive Positioning:**
- All current devices: No sensing capability
- **Our Play:** "Precision thrombectomy" — data-driven device selection

**Regulatory Path:** De Novo
**Timeline:** 24-36 months
**Near-term Viable:** ✅ YES — sensor integration into existing catheter

**Risk:** Sensor cost, clinical validation required
