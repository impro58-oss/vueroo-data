# FINTENDANT — SOM Stress Test Analysis
**Serviceable Obtainable Market: Scenario Modeling**
**Date:** March 24, 2026  
**Concept:** FINTENDANT (AI Finance Manager)  
**Analyst:** Lumina  
**Purpose:** Validate $480M-$1.2B SOM claim with multi-scenario stress testing

---

## Executive Summary

**Original Claim:** $480M - $1.2B SOM (Years 1-5)  
**Confidence Level:** MEDIUM-HIGH — defensible with execution-dependent caveats

**Bottom Line:** The base case SOM of **$480M-$600M** is achievable with competent execution. The optimistic $1.2B case requires exceptional performance across multiple variables. The bear case drops to $70M-$120M if trust barriers prove insurmountable.

## Methodology

**SOM Definition:** Realistic revenue capture in Years 1-5 from the US market initially, expanding to UK/EU in Years 3-5.

**Key Input Variables:**
1. Target market size (US households with multiple credit cards/subscriptions)
2. Market growth rate (AI adoption in personal finance)
3. Platform conversion rate (FINTENDANT penetration)
4. Average revenue per user (ARPU)
5. Time to scale (ramp-up curve)
6. Geographic expansion timing (US → UK → EU)

## Key Risks to SOM Achievement

### Risk 1: Trust Deficit (HIGH IMPACT)
**Scenario:** Despite market readiness, users won't give AI execution authority over their money.

**Mitigation:**
- Tiered permission model (advise → execute with approval → full auto)
- Insurance and error protection fund
- Complete transparency and audit trails
- Start with "savings finder" (low risk), graduate to "bill negotiator," then "full manager"

### Risk 2: Regulatory Freeze (MEDIUM-HIGH IMPACT)
**Scenario:** CFPB or state regulators classify autonomous money management as "investment advice" requiring licenses.

**Mitigation:**
- Partner with existing RIAs/banks (license piggybacking)
- Structure as "optimization software" not "advice"
- Regulatory sandbox participation from day one
- Legal opinion on classification before launch

### Risk 3: Bank API Resistance (MEDIUM IMPACT)
**Scenario:** Banks limit API access or charge prohibitive fees for autonomous agents.

**Mitigation:**
- Multi-provider strategy (Plaid + MX + Yodlee + direct APIs)
- Negotiate enterprise rates early
- Build direct bank partnerships
- Fallback to screen scraping (less elegant but functional)

### Risk 4: Incumbent Response (MEDIUM IMPACT)
**Scenario:** Chase, Capital One, or Intuit launch competing autonomous features.

**Mitigation:**
- Speed to market (6-9 month head start)
- Multi-bank neutrality (not tied to one provider)
- Superior AI (continuous learning, cross-bank optimization)
- Data moat (user behavior patterns improve recommendations)

### Risk 5: Economic Downturn (LOW-MEDIUM IMPACT)
**Scenario:** Recession reduces discretionary spending, users cut subscriptions.

**Mitigation:**
- Counter-cyclical value prop (saves money, justifies cost)
- Free tier for hardship cases
- Actually benefits from downturn (more deal-seeking behavior)

## Validation Checklist

Before claiming $480M-$1.2B SOM is achievable, verify:

- [ ] **Customer Acquisition:** Can we acquire users at CAC <$100?
- [ ] **Trust Building:** Do 30%+ of beta users grant execution authority within 30 days?
- [ ] **Retention:** Is annual churn <25% by Year 2?
- [ ] **Unit Economics:** Is LTV/CAC > 5:1 at scale?
- [ ] **Savings Proof:** Do users save 5x the subscription cost in Year 1?
- [ ] **Regulatory Stability:** Can we operate without investment advisor licenses for 12+ months?
- [ ] **Bank API Access:** Are major banks (Chase, BofA, Citi) accessible via API?
- [ ] **Viral Coefficient:** Is K-factor > 0.3 (each user brings 0.3+ new users)?

**If 6+ boxes checked:** Optimistic case probable  
**If 4-5 boxes checked:** Base case likely  
**If <4 boxes checked:** Bear case — reconsider opportunity

## Recommendations for Stockward

### Investment Decision Framework

**If pursuing FINTENDANT as venture studio build:**
- Require proof of 5%+ conversion rate in closed beta (1,000 user pilot) before Series A
- Cap initial commitment at $5M pre-seed (validates SOM assumptions)
- Structure milestone-based vesting: 25% equity at 3% conversion, 50% at 5%, 75% at 8%+
- Partner with existing fintech infrastructure (Plaid, banking-as-a-service)

**If evaluating as sourced deal:**
- Treat $480M-$1.2B SOM as "base case optimistic" — discount to $300M-$500M for valuation
- Focus due diligence on: (1) actual user trust metrics, (2) regulatory pathway, (3) bank API access
- Walk away if unit economics don't show LTV/CAC > 3:1 by Month 18

### Red Flags to Monitor

1. **CAC inflation** — If customer acquisition costs exceed $150, unit economics collapse
2. **Regulatory reclassification** — If CFPB requires RIA licenses, pivot to B2B/white-label
3. **Bank API restrictions** — If major banks block access, competitive moat erodes
4. **Incumbent fast-follow** — If Chase/Intuit launch within 12 months, first-mover advantage lost
5. **Trust plateau** — If >50% of users refuse execution authority by Month 6, product-market fit questionable
