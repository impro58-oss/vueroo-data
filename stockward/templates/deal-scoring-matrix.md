## Scoring Methodology

### Criteria (adjust weights per sector)

| Rank | Criterion | Importance | Weight | Rationale |
|------|-----------|------------|--------|-----------|
| 1 | **Market Attractiveness** | 10 | 25% | TAM, growth rate, competitive dynamics |
| 2 | **Strategic Fit** | 9 | 22.5% | Alignment with Stockward thesis and network |
| 3 | **Team Quality** | 9 | 22.5% | Founder experience, execution capability |
| 4 | **Traction/Metrics** | 8 | 20% | Revenue, growth, unit economics |
| 5 | **Risk Profile** | 7 | 10% | Regulatory, market, execution risks |

*Adjust weights based on sector and stage. Total weight = 100%*

### Scoring Scale: 1-10
- **10:** Exceptional / best-in-class / unicorn potential
- **8-9:** Strong / above average / clear opportunity
- **6-7:** Good / competitive / viable
- **4-5:** Adequate / average / concerns exist
- **1-3:** Below average / major red flags

## Methodology Notes

**Origin:** This framework was ported from competitive analysis of medical devices ([[Framing Coil Competitive Scoring Matrix]]), demonstrating that rigorous evaluation frameworks are domain-agnostic.

**Key Insight:** Weighted scoring forces disciplined trade-off analysis. A company with exceptional team (10) but weak market (4) scores differently than one with strong market (8) and good team (7).

**Customization:** Adjust weights and criteria for:
- **Early-stage:** Increase Team Quality weight (founder matters more)
- **Growth-stage:** Increase Traction/Metrics weight (proven model matters)
- **Deep tech:** Increase Risk Profile scrutiny (regulatory, technical)
