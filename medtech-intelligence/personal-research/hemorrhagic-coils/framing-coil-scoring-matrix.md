## DETAILED SCORING RATIONALE

### 1. STRYKER TARGET TETRA — 93.5% ★★★★★
**Overall Winner - Best Balance of Stability + Softness**

| Criterion | Score | Rationale |
|-----------|-------|-----------|
| Framing Stability | 10 | Tetrahedral shape = exceptional 3D stability; enhanced shape memory; gold standard |
| Placement Control | 9 | Smooth deployment, excellent microcatheter stability, predictable placement |
| Neck Support | 9 | Uniform distribution with concentric filling; excellent neck coverage |
| Packing Foundation | 10 | "Frame to finish" capability; stable basket for all subsequent coils |
| Prolapse Resistance | 9 | Stable tetrahedral geometry resists herniation |

**Key Strengths:**
- **Softest coil with enhanced shape stability** — unique combination
- Tetrahedral shape provides multi-planar stability
- 12x softer than standard coils yet maintains structural integrity
- Frame-to-finish capability reduces need for multiple coil types

**Weaknesses:**
- Premium pricing
- May be "too soft" for very large/wide-neck aneurysms

---

### 2. MICROVENTION COMPASS — 86.5% ★★★★☆
**Best for Firm, Stable Framing**

| Criterion | Score | Rationale |
|-----------|-------|-----------|
| Framing Stability | 9 | "More firm, does not collapse, resists compartmentalization" — designed specifically for stability |
| Placement Control | 9 | Reduced catheter movement during deployment; excellent control |
| Neck Support | 8 | Good neck coverage; variable diameter loops apposed to periphery |
| Packing Foundation | 9 | Versatile framing for all aneurysm morphologies; stable basket formation |
| Prolapse Resistance | 8 | Firm construction resists prolapse better than softer coils |

**Key Strengths:**
- **Explicitly designed as "framing coil"** — not compromised by dual-purpose design
- Firmness = superior stability in wide-neck aneurysms
- Longer lengths available (good for large aneurysms)
- Catheter stability during deployment

**Weaknesses:**
- Less conformable than softer options
- May not be ideal for very small aneurysms
- No hydrogel option in Compass line

---

### 3. MEDTRONIC AXIUM PRIME FC — 83.5% ★★★★☆
**Best Softness + Neck Coverage Balance**

| Criterion | Score | Rationale |
|-----------|-------|-----------|
| Framing Stability | 8 | Good stability but prioritizes softness; closed loop design |
| Placement Control | 9 | **Highest pliability** (lowest AWN) = gentle deployment; minimal wall stress |
| Neck Support | 9 | **"Consistent neck coverage"** — closed loops reduce herniation |
| Packing Foundation | 8 | Stable frame but softer than competitors |
| Prolapse Resistance | 8 | Closed loops specifically designed to reduce implant herniation |

**Key Strengths:**
- **Softest deployment profile** — minimal risk of aneurysm rupture during placement
- Closed loop geometry specifically for neck support
- Frame → Finish product family consistency
- Superior pliability vs Target coils (clinical studies)

**Weaknesses:**
- May lack firmness for very complex/wide-neck aneurysms
- Stability trades off with softness

---

### 4. STRYKER TARGET 3D — 83.5% ★★★★☆
**Tried-and-True Classic**

| Criterion | Score | Rationale |
|-----------|-------|-----------|
| Framing Stability | 9 | Alternating large/small loops create stable frame; proven design |
| Placement Control | 8 | Rotates 90° at each loop; can be less predictable than Tetra |
| Neck Support | 8 | Good wall apposition; less optimized for neck than Tetra |
| Packing Foundation | 9 | Established stable basket performance |
| Prolapse Resistance | 8 | Good but not exceptional |

**Key Strengths:**
- **Longest clinical track record** — 10+ years of data
- Proven 3D design with alternating loops
- Broad size range available
- Well-understood deployment characteristics

**Weaknesses:**
- Being superseded by Target Tetra technology
- Less soft than newer competitors
- No frame-to-finish capability

---

### 5. MICROVENTION HYDROFRAME — 83.5% ★★★★☆
**Best for Volume Fill + Neck Recurrence Prevention**

| Criterion | Score | Rationale |
|-----------|-------|-----------|
| Framing Stability | 8 | Hydrogel expands 4-9x for mechanical stability; may soften over time |
| Placement Control | 7 | Hydrogel expansion adds complexity; longer procedure time |
| Neck Support | 10 | **Hydrogel at neck reduces recurrence** — proven clinical benefit |
| Packing Foundation | 9 | Greater volume fill = denser packing foundation |
| Prolapse Resistance | 8 | Volume expansion provides mechanical resistance |

**Key Strengths:**
- **Unique hydrogel technology** — 4-9x volume expansion
- Superior neck healing (clinical evidence: 5.9% vs 39.4% recurrence)
- Greater volume than bare platinum framing coils
- Unique in market — no direct competitor

**Weaknesses:**
- Requires different deployment technique (expansion factor)
- Longer procedure times
- Potential for delayed complications (edema, inflammation)
- Premium pricing

### 6. KANEKA i-ED COMPLEX — 73.5% ★★★☆☆
**Emerging Alternative**

| Criterion | Score | Rationale |
|-----------|-------|-----------|
| Framing Stability | 7 | Low-memory shape conforms but may lack firmness for wide-neck |
| Placement Control | 8 | SilkySoft profile (3x softer than Target 360 Ultra) = gentle |

- Japanese innovation, growing US presence

**Weaknesses:**
- Newer to market = less clinical evidence
- Softer profile may compromise framing in challenging cases
- Limited market share/data

## STRATEGIC RECOMMENDATIONS BY USE CASE

### Wide-Neck Aneurysms (High Complexity)
**Winner:** MicroVention Compass
**Runner-up:** Stryker Target Tetra
**Rationale:** Firmness and stability are paramount

### Small Aneurysms (<5mm)
**Winner:** Stryker Target Tetra
**Runner-up:** Medtronic Axium Prime FC
**Rationale:** Softness + stability balance critical

### Ruptured Aneurysms (Delicate Tissue)
**Winner:** Medtronic Axium Prime FC
**Runner-up:** Stryker Target Tetra
**Rationale:** Softest deployment minimizes rupture risk

### Large/Giant Aneurysms
**Winner:** MicroVention HydroFrame
**Runner-up:** MicroVention Compass
**Rationale:** Volume expansion + longer lengths available

### High Recurrence Risk (Neck-Heavy)
**Winner:** MicroVention HydroFrame
**Runner-up:** Medtronic Axium Prime FC
**Rationale:** Hydrogel at neck proven to reduce recurrence

### Frame-to-Finish Efficiency
**Winner:** Stryker Target Tetra
**Runner-up:** MicroVention Compass
**Rationale:** Tetra can serve as framing + filling in small spaces

## KEY INSIGHTS

1. **Target Tetra is the clear winner** — best balance of all criteria, especially the critical "Packing Foundation" criterion

2. **No single coil dominates all categories** — strategic selection based on aneurysm morphology is essential

3. **Hydrogel (HydroFrame) is unique** — only product scoring 10 on neck support due to proven clinical outcomes

4. **Stryker has portfolio depth** — Tetra (premium) + 3D (classic) covers market segments

5. **Medtronic leads on softness** — Axium Prime FC is the "gentlest" framing coil

6. **MicroVention offers differentiation** — Compass (firm) and HydroFrame (volume) serve different needs

7. **Kaneka is emerging** — i-ED offers soft conformability but lacks framing-specific optimization
