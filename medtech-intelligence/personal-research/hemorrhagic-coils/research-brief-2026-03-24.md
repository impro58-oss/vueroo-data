## Coil Types Explained

### 1. FRAMING COILS
**Purpose:** Create initial stable basket/framework inside the aneurysm
**Characteristics:** 3D/complex shape, larger loops, stiffer, deployed first
**Function:** Establishes the "cage" that prevents coil migration and provides structure

### 2. FILLING COILS  
**Purpose:** Pack inside the framing coil basket to increase occlusion density
**Characteristics:** Softer, helical/standard shapes, progressively sized
**Function:** Maximizes packing density within the frame

### 3. FINISHING COILS
**Purpose:** Dense packing of remaining small spaces, especially at the neck
**Characteristics:** Ultra-soft, smallest diameters (1-1.5mm), Nano sizes
**Function:** Complete occlusion, prevent recurrence

## Coil Selection Strategy (Clinical Context)

### Typical Deployment Sequence:
1. **Framing Coil** → Creates stable basket (e.g., Target 3D, Axium Prime FC, Compass)
2. **Filling Coils** → Pack inside frame (e.g., Target 360, HyperSoft, standard helical)
3. **Finishing Coils** → Dense pack neck/small spaces (e.g., Target Nano, Axium Prime ES, HydroSoft)

### By Aneurysm Type:
- **Small aneurysms (<5mm):** Target Nano, Target Ultra series
- **Wide-neck/complex:** MicroPlex Complex, Compass, 3D coils
- **Oblong aneurysms:** VFC (MicroVention) can be used as framing
- **Large/giant aneurysms:** Flow diversion + coiling, HydroCoil for volume

## Key Takeaways

1. **Stryker dominates** with the broadest portfolio and strongest clinical evidence (GDC legacy + Target innovations)

2. **Medtronic competes on softness/plability** — Axium Prime series emphasizes gentle deployment

3. **MicroVention leads innovation** — hydrogel technology (HydroCoil) is unique differentiator

4. **Coil selection is anatomical** — framing/filling/finishing isn't brand-specific but morphology-specific

5. **US market is oligopolistic** — top 3 players control ~60-75%, making it difficult for new entrants

6. **Technology evolution:** Moving toward softer coils, better deployment control, and adjunctive technologies (stents, flow diversion)
